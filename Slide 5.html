State-of-the-art and Gaps

Chen et al., 2017
  • ✅

    GSR Monitoring: Included galvanic skin response

  • ✅

    Heart Rate Monitoring: Included heart rate sensing

  • ✅

    Ambient/Body Temperature: Included temperature sensing

  • ❌

    Automated Cooling: No cooling mechanism

  • ❌

    Multi-sensor Fusion: Sensors used independently

  • ❌

    Early Proactive Intervention: Reactive approach

Khan et al., 2023
  • ✅

    Core Temperature Trigger: Single-point detection

  • ❌

    GSR/Heart Rate: Did not consider these early warning signs

  • ❌

    Early Detection: Cooling only starts when core temp is already too high

  • ✅

    Automated Cooling: Included cooling mechanism

  • ❌

    Multi-sensor Fusion: Single sensor approach

  • ❌

    Risk of Heat Stress Damage: Still possible due to late activation

State-of-the-art Gap: Existing solutions either use multiple sensors without automation (Chen) or provide cooling too late (Khan). Both approaches lack proactive intervention through multi-sensor fusion, creating risk of heat stress damage before prevention systems activate.
